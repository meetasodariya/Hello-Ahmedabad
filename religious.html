Religious Places In AHMEDABAD

Where Faith Finds Its Home and Hearts Find Peace

Ahmedabad, a city rich in history and culture, is home to a diverse array of religious places that reflect its harmonious blend of traditions and beliefs. The city's religious landscape offers a glimpse into its past and its present, attracting both pilgrims and tourists alike.


One of the most iconic landmarks in Ahmedabad is the Sabarmati Ashram, where Mahatma Gandhi resided during India's struggle for independence. This ashram stands as a testament to Gandhi's philosophy of non-violence and simplicity. Its tranquil surroundings and historic significance make it a revered place for reflection and inspiration.


Ahmedabad's religious fabric is further enriched by its Jain temples, such as the Hathee Singh Jain Temple, which boasts an awe-inspiring marble structure adorned with intricate carvings.


With its spiritual ambiance, Ahmedabad's religious places transcend mere architectural beauty, offering visitors a profound sense of connection to the city's historical and spiritual roots. Let us explore the religious places of Ahmedabad.

About Vaishno Devi Temple in Ahmedabad

The Vaishno Devi Temple in Ahmedabad stands as a spiritual oasis, drawing devotees from near and far to pay homage to the revered deity. Nestled amidst the bustling city, this temple offers a sanctuary of devotion and tranquility. With its ornate architecture and vibrant religious ceremonies, the temple provides a place for worshipers to immerse themselves in the divine aura and seek solace in their faith.



Hutheesing Jain Temple:

Address:

Hutheesing Jain Temple, Camp Rd., Bardolpura, Madhupura, Ahmedabad, Gujarat 380004


About Hutheesing Jain Temple

Another most beautiful and prominent religious place in Ahmedabad, Hutheesing Jain Temple was built by Seth Hutheesingh 1850 AD. Dedicated to 15th Jain Trithanakara Dharmnath the double-storied white marble building the temple is truly a must see monument. The temple is surrounded by 52 shrines each shrine decorated with an image of Tirthankara.

About ISKCON TEMPLE

International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) temple built in vast area with number of trees and tranquil environment is a best place to visit. Also called as the Hare Krishna Temple it is dedicated to Lord Krishna and his wife Radha. The temple holds prayers both in mornings and evenings. The best time to visit the temple is when it celebrates two main festivals, the Janmashtami and Nand Mahotsav which are related to Lord Krishna. The temple can also be visited on other days.

Swami Narayan Temple-Kalupur:

Address:

Swaminarayan Mandir Road, Old City, Kalupur, Ahmedabad, Gujarat 380001
Website: https://www.swaminarayan.in/


About Swami Narayan Temple-Kalupur

It is believed to be the first Narayan Swamy temple built in 1822; the temple is located in Kalupur in Ahmedabad. The interiors and exteriors of the temple are beautifully designed in Gujarati style and the temple also consists of nine tombs which are famously called as 'Nau Gaz Pir' or Nine Yard Saints. Burmese teak is used in carving the designs and the temple has beautiful sculptures of Lord Swami Narayan.



Modhera Sun Temple:



Address:

H4MM+95W, On, Mehsana - Becharaji Rd, Highway, Modhera, Gujarat 384212



About Modhera Sun Temple

Most famous for its structural design Modhera Sun temple is one more beautiful temple to visit. The temple consists of three chambers which are Guda Mandap, Surya Kund and Sabha Mandap each with a specific reason behind it. Guda Mandap is the main sanctum sanctorum; Surya Kund is a tank which is beautifully carved whereas Sabha Mandap is a place where all religious matters are discussed.

Address:

Bhadra Fort, Bhadra Rd, Old City, Bhadra, Ahmedabad, Gujarat 380001

About Bhadrakali Temple

Located in the Bhadra Fort built in 1411 with lush green gardens and palaces the Bhadrakali Temple is also most famous in Ahmedabad. The temple is built by the Marathas during the Aazam Khan rule. The temple is richly decorated and worshipped during the Navarathri festival season.



Jama Masjid:



Address:

2HFP+HR5, Manek Chowk, Gandhi Rd, Danapidth, Khadia, Ahmedabad, Gujarat 380001



About Jama Masjid

Jama Masjid, located in the heart of Ahmedabad, stands as an architectural marvel and a testament to the city's historical and cultural legacy. Built in the 15th century, this grand mosque showcases exquisite Indo-Saracenic design, with its striking domes, intricately carved minarets, and splendid central courtyard. As a spiritual hub for Muslims, it serves as a place of congregational prayers and fosters a sense of community.

About St Mary's Orthodox Cathedral Church

St. Mary's Orthodox Cathedral Church in Ahmedabad stands as a cherished symbol of the Orthodox Christian community's faith and heritage. Beyond its religious significance, the cathedral also contributes to the city's diverse religious tapestry, embodying the spirit of unity amidst diversity that Ahmedabad is known for.
