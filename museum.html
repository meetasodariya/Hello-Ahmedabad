Museums of AHMEDABAD

Explore Huge Heritage of Ahmedabad.

Ahmedabad, a vibrant city in the western state of Gujarat, is home to a rich cultural heritage that is beautifully showcased through its museums. The city boasts a diverse range of museums that cater to various interests and provide a captivating glimpse into its history, art, and traditions.


For history enthusiasts, the Sabarmati Ashram stands as a testament to the ideals of Mahatma Gandhi, the father of the Indian nation. This peaceful abode served as Gandhi's residence during the struggle for India's independence and now operates as a museum that showcases his personal belongings, letters, and photographs.


Ahmedabad's museums also celebrate its artistic and architectural brilliance. The Sanskar Kendra Museum, designed by the iconic architect Le Corbusier, is a modernist marvel that showcases the city's history, art, and culture.


The museum's innovative design and carefully curated exhibits make it a must-visit for those interested in exploring the city's evolving artistic expressions and urban development. In Ahmedabad, these museums collectively weave a tapestry that illuminates the city's past, present, and future. Now let us see some of the famous Museums.



Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel National Museum:

Address:

Gandhi-Sardar Smruthi Chowk, Opp. Circuit House, Shahibag, Ahmedabad, Gujarat
Opening hours: 9:30 am to 5 pm, on all days of the week except Mondays.
Timings for the 3D Interactive show is 7 pm and 7:45 pm.
Entry fee: INR 20/person

About Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel National Museum

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel National Museum is certainly one of the famous museums in Ahmedabad, visited by numerable tourists round the year. Established in 1978 by Shri Babubhai Jashbhai Patel; this museum has a gorgeous display of items and artifacts depicting the life and achievements of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.



Calico Museum Of Textiles:

Address:

Location: The Retreat, Airport Rd, Opp. Rani Sati Mandir, Jain Colony, Shahibag, Ahmedabad, Gujarat
Opening hours: On all days of the week except Wednesdays; from 10 am to 12:30 pm and 2:45 pm to 4:30 pm.
Entry fee: Free


About Calico Museum Of Textiles

The Calico Museum of Textiles is popular for its astounding collections of varied kinds of textiles, artifacts, antique fabrics, and apparel. The unique and most intriguing collection of Kashmiri shawls and tye and dye materials looks supremely vibrant and entices every mind. This is not only a famous textile museum in Ahmedabad run by the Sarabhai Foundation but one of the oldest textile museums in the World.



Sanskar Kendra:



Address:

Location: Bhagtacharya Road, Near Sardar Patel, Bridge, Paldi, Ahmedabad, Gujarat
Opening hours: 10 am to 6 pm
Entry fee: Free



About Sanskar Kendra

Established in 1956, this is one of the oldest yet famous museums in Ahmedabad. Designed by renowned architect Le Corbusie; this place welcomes visitors on all days of the week to see the art, culture, history, and heritage of the city. This is one of the most significant places to discover the traits and traditions of the bygone days, practiced by native people.

Vechaar Utensils Museum:

Address:

Opp. Vasna Tol Naka, Ahmedabad, Gujarat 380055
Opening hours: 1 pm to 3 pm and 5 pm to 10:30 pm. This place is open every day except Mondays.
Entry fee: INR 20/person


About Vechaar Utensils Museum

Situated right opposite to Vasna Toll Naka; Vechaar Utensils Museum is surely one of its kind. Depicting 4500 plus samples of century-old and obsolete pots, potteries and utensils used once in various parts of the country, this place stands out amongst the famous museums in Ahmedabad in both concept and collections. If anyone is interested to know and discover the evolution of utensils with mankind; there is no better place than this.



Auto World Vintage Car Museum:



Address:

Dastan Estate Sardar Patel Ring Road Kathwada, Ahmedabad, Gujarat
Opening hours: 8 am to 9 pm
Entry fee: INR 50/person



About Auto World Vintage Car Museum

A must-visit for all car lovers, this world vintage car museum Ahmedabad boasts of has maximum footfall in a day. Nestled in Dastan Estate, this museum displays outstanding collections of antique vehicles, carriages, buggies and utility vehicles. From romantic convertibles to railway saloons, from shooting brake cars to grand limos- the place has everything worth seeing. This open-air car museum Ahmedabad city houses should not be missed at any cost.

Lalbhai Dalpatbhai Museum:

Address:

Opposite Gujarat University, Navarangpura, Ahmedabad.
Opening hours: 10: 30 am to 5: 30 pm. This museum is closed on Mondays.
Entry fee: Free


About Lalbhai Dalpatbhai Museum

With vivid collections of Indian sculptures, Mughal paintings, manuscripts of ancient Jain literature, miniatures, bronze statues, wood sculptures, bead work items, and antique coins; Lalbhai Dalpatbhai Museum holds a significant place in the group of famous museums in Ahmedabad. Other than this, there is an Art Reference Library and Conservation Laboratory which preserves artifact, documents, manuscripts and archives of the country`s history and heritage.



Kites Museum:



Address:

Sankar Kender, Bhagtacharya Road, Kocharab, Paldi, Ahmedabad, Gujarat
Opening hours: 10 am-12 noon and 4 pm to 6 pm every day except Monday.
Entry fee: Free



About Kites Museum

Popular as Patang Kite Museum, this is another unique place in the city and one of the prime tourist attractions. Established in 1954, this concept of the display was pioneered by Bhanu Shah who donated all this personal collection of kites and set up this place. The kite museum Ahmedabad city flaunts of exhibits an astounding collection of kites and papers used to make them. From bright-looking Japanese kites to mirror work and block print kites- the collection is rich and quite interesting. If you happen to be here, don`t miss to see the 16 feet long kite that depicts Garba Dance and Radha Krishna images.

Shreyas Folk Museum:

Address:

Shreyas Tekra Rd, Opposite, Mithila Society, Bhudarpura, Ayojan Nagar, Ahmedabad, Gujarat
Opening hours: 9 am to 2 pm. The museum is open on all days of the week except Sundays.
Entry fee: INR 10/person


About Shreyas Folk Museum

If you wish to glance at the city`s heritage, culture, and traditions at one go; Shreyas Folk Museum is one of the very few museums in Ahmedabad that serve the purpose. With 4 sprawling sections namely Lokayatan Folk Museum, Kalpana Mangaldas Balayatan Museum, Kathani and Sangeet Vadyakhand; this museum is known for its exhibits ranging from art and craft of Gujarati communities like Charan, Ahir, Kathi and Rabari, embroidery and bead work, woodcarving, tie dyed materials, toys, puppets, masks, costumes and coins.



Archer Art Gallery:



Address:

Girish Cold Drinks, 2nd Floor, Phoenix Opposite New, Ahmedabad, Gujarat
Opening hours: 10:30 am to 7 pm. The museum is open to visitors on all days of the week except Sundays.
Entry fee: Free



About Archer Art Gallery

Another name in the list of popular museums in Ahmedabad is none other than Archer Art Gallery. This is not only a museum but also one of the most renowned art galleries of India displaying astounding paintings, sculptures, and graphics. The exclusive and well-preserved collection has not only enhanced the fame of the place but has also helped in winning many national and international accolades and recognitions from world famous artists like M F Hussain, Amit Ambalal and Bhupen Khakhar.

Varso Toy Museum:

Address:

1765 Dhobini Pole Haveli No, Ahmedabad, Gujarat
Opening hours: 11 am to 4 pm
Entry fee: Free


About Varso Toy Museum

This is a 400-years old heritage haveli known as Jethabhai ni Haveli, now converted to a wonderland for kids. Managed and run by Varso Foundation; this one of its kind toy museum Ahmedabad has, exhibits various types of classic to contemporary toys. From toy cars, dolls, blocks to wooden toys- this place attracts kids and adults alike and depicts the evolution of toys and play with dynamic Indian culture.

About Vikram Sarabhai Space Exhibition

Vikram Sarabhai Space Exhibition (VSSE) is housed at Ahmedabad, which happens to be the hometown of Dr. Sarabhai. This exhibition is an attempt to share with the general public our achievements, hopes and concerns through the media of working models, live panels, static displays, the 3D theatre and the interactive multimedia presentations.
