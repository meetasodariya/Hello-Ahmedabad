Introduction

Ahmedabad is known as the “Manchester of India”.

About Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad is the seventh largest metropolis of India. The city is being rated as the fastest growing city in India.With the population of approx 4.5 million, the city is stated as the center of commerce, and is the home to a large population of traders.

How to Reach Ahmedabad?

Ahmedabad is a very well connected city, connected to almost all big cities in Gujarat. The railway station in Ahmedabad is connected to a number of major cities that include Mumbai and Delhi. Some of the important trains of Ahmedabad are Gujarat Express, Saurashtra Express, Sabarmati Express, etc.

Geography of Ahmedabad

As for geography of Ahmedabad, the city is located on the western side of India, on the banks of Sabarmati River. The region witnesses dry climate during summer and most of the part of the city is desert. This results in extreme hot climate. Therefore, it is advised to visit the city during winter season.

History

The history of Ahmedabad is centuries old.

The Foundation of Ahmedabad:

Ahmedabad, a vibrant city in Gujarat, India, has a rich history that dates back to the 15th century. It was founded by Sultan Ahmed Shah in 1411 AD.Ahmedabad's foundation marked the beginning of a flourishing era, attracting traders, artisans, and settlers from various regions, contributing to its diverse and inclusive character.

Ahmedabad's Role in the Independence Movement:

During the British colonial rule, Ahmedabad played a significant role in India's struggle for independence. The city became a hotbed of political activism, with prominent leaders like Mahatma Gandhi residing here. It served as a prominent center for the Non-Cooperation and Civil Disobedience Movements.

Ahmedabad's Urban Development and Heritage:

Over the years, Ahmedabad has witnessed extensive urban development while still preserving its historical heritage. The city boasts an amalgamation of old-world charm and modern infrastructure.Additionally, Ahmedabad's status as India's first UNESCO World Heritage City (declared in 2017).
